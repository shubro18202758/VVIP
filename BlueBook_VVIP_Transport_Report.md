# **1\. Overview: What Is the Blue Book?**

The Blue Book is the Union Ministry of Home Affairs' (MHA) classified security protocol manual governing all aspects of VVIP protection and transport in India. It is the foundational legal and operational document for the Special Protection Group (SPG) and all agencies involved in protecting the nation's highest-ranking officials.

A companion document, the Yellow Book, covers VIP security for ministers, governors, and other public figures. Together, these two manuals constitute India's comprehensive VVIP and VIP security architecture.

## **1.1 Scope and Authority**

* Covers the President, Vice President, Prime Minister and their immediate families

* Governs visiting foreign Heads of State and dignitaries

* Issued and periodically updated by the MHA in coordination with intelligence agencies

* Legally backed by the Special Protection Group Act (1988, amended 2019\)

* Binding on all agencies: SPG, NSG, ITBP, CRPF, CISF, and all State Police forces

## **1.2 Historical Context**

The concept of formalised VVIP security emerged in the 1960s when Naxalism began posing a serious threat to political leaders. The SPG was formed in 1985 following the assassination of Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, and the Blue Book was codified to incorporate new proximate security protocols. The manual has been updated to reflect assassination of PM Rajiv Gandhi in 1991 and more recently to address threats like IEDs, signal-triggered devices, cyber espionage, and deepfakes.

# **2\. Security Tier Classification System**

India's VVIP and VIP security is organised into six tiers based on threat perception assessed by a committee of Intelligence Bureau, MHA, and state government officials. Each tier specifies the number of personnel, the agencies deployed, vehicle count, and nature of protection.

| Category | Personnel | Agency | Profile |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **SPG** | 40-60+ | SPG (Cabinet Sec.) | Prime Minister only |
| **Z+** | 55 | NSG \+ Police | High-risk: top political leaders, ex-PMs |
| **Z** | 22 | NSG/ITBP/CRPF | Moderate-high: ministers, senior officials |
| **Y+** | 11 | Commandos \+ Police | Moderate: judges, state ministers |
| **Y** | 8 | Commandos \+ Police | Low-moderate: senior bureaucrats, public figures |
| **X** | 2 | Armed Police | Minimal: minor threat individuals |

Note: The SPG has classified details and exclusively protects the Prime Minister. As of 2026, PM Narendra Modi is the sole SPG protectee under the amended 2019 Act. Security decisions for all other categories are made after a formal threat assessment by the Central Security Agency.

# **3\. Road Journey Protocols — The Core Transport Framework**

Road transport protocols are the most detailed and operationally critical section of the Blue Book. Every convoy movement is governed by strict procedural rules from pre-departure checks to post-arrival debrief. The following sub-sections detail every layer of the protocol.

## **3.1 The Security Box — Convoy Structure**

The fundamental unit of VVIP road transport is the 'security box' — a fixed formation of vehicles that travel together as a tightly controlled unit. The PM's motorcade typically consists of a minimum of five vehicles, scaling up depending on the threat environment.

| Position | Vehicle | Role & Function |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **1** | Pilot Warning Car | State police pilot; road clearance, lights/sirens, crowd control signal |
| **2** | Technical/ECM Car | Electronic Counter Measures; signal jamming, IED blocking, comms relay |
| **3** | VVIP Primary Car | Armoured (VR10 level for PM); houses protectee \+ SPG proximate team |
| **4** | Escort Car I | Armed SPG/security personnel, close counter-assault team |
| **5** | Escort Car II | Second armed layer; same make and colour as primary cars |
| **6** | Spare/Decoy Car | Identical to VVIP car; contingency vehicle for emergency switch |
| **7** | Ambulance | Medical team on standby; Mercedes-Benz Sprinter for PM convoy |
| **8+** | SSP / DM / IB Cars | Local authority officials; trailing the core security box |

Critical Rule: All vehicles in the security box should preferably be of the same make and colour to prevent easy identification of the VVIP vehicle by potential threats. Where mandated by scale, both the primary VVIP car and the spare car must be bulletproof. For the PM, the current standard is the Mercedes-Maybach S650 Guard with VR10-level ballistic protection.

## **3.2 Pre-Journey Preparation Protocols**

**Advance Security Liaison (ASL)**

Days before any visit, the SPG convenes an ASL meeting with all responsible agencies. This is a multi-agency coordination session that includes:

* SPG leadership and proximate protection officers

* State DGP and relevant Superintendent of Police (SSP)

* District Magistrate (DM) of the area being visited

* Intelligence Bureau (IB) representatives

* Local intelligence officials

The ASL covers route finalisation, vulnerable point identification, contingency planning, threat intelligence briefing, and communication protocols.

**Route Survey and Anti-Sabotage Checks**

* A thorough physical and technical check of the entire route is conducted

* All culverts, bridges, drains, and underpasses are physically inspected

* All unattended/unmarked vehicles on the route must be identified and removed

* High-rise buildings along the route are identified; personnel are posted at vantage points

* Snipers are deployed on rooftops at critical junctures

* Sniffer dogs, explosive detectors, and metal detectors are used for anti-sabotage checks

**Vehicle Verification**

* All convoy vehicles undergo thorough mechanical and anti-sabotage checks before departure

* Driver antecedents are verified; only experienced and vetted drivers are used

* Seating plans are finalised well in advance and cannot be changed without SPG approval

* Drivers are briefed on speed synchronisation so all vehicles maintain convoy formation

* The National Flag is fender-mounted on the driver's side of the VVIP vehicle

## **3.3 During Transit — Operational Rules**

* The SPG provides proximate security at all times with 40-60 officers in close proximity to the PM

* Electronic countermeasures (ECM/jamming) are active throughout the journey to block IED triggers

* The convoy moves only after the SPG Director receives clearance from the state police that the route is secured

* State police sanitise the route and remain deployed along it throughout the journey

* The SPG's 'go' signal is mandatory before the convoy departs any location

* The convoy must not stop except at the planned destination — any unplanned halt is treated as a security emergency

## **3.4 Traffic and Crowd Management**

The Blue Book contains explicit instructions for managing the civilian environment around the convoy route to minimise disruption while ensuring VVIP safety.

* Traffic points are manned by traffic police and reinforced at heavy intersections

* Traffic is regulated, not stopped, unless stoppage is unavoidable for VVIP safety

* If traffic must be stopped, it is halted at a distance from the intersection, not at it

* Streets may be declared temporarily closed or converted from two-way to one-way

* After the convoy passes, traffic arrangements must be maintained until congestion normalises

* The road is divided into sectors, each under a designated officer responsible for crowd and traffic

* Security personnel face the crowd, not the VVIP — a fundamental crowd-management principle

* Plainclothes officers are embedded in crowds at public functions, some posing as party workers

## **3.5 Contingency Planning — Plan B Requirements**

One of the most critical requirements of the Blue Book is mandatory contingency planning. The first chapter's fifth rule states that state governments must implement protective arrangements including a ready-to-activate Plan B at all times.

* An alternate route must always be pre-planned, sanitised, and secured with personnel

* Contingency hospitals and safe houses are identified along all routes and officers are briefed

* Contingency vehicle formations (contingency motorcades) are arranged at halting locations

* If the primary mode of transport (e.g. helicopter) is unavailable due to weather, a secure road route must be ready

* All contingency plans are discussed and rehearsed during the ASL — including a physical trial run of alternate routes

# **4\. Non-Road Transport Protocols**

## **4.1 Air Travel**

* Sufficient uniformed police are posted at airports and helipads during VVIP arrival and departure

* Plainclothes officers are deployed in addition to uniformed security

* The airfield entrance must be cleared of all unauthorised persons

* Landing grounds must be cleared of any obstructions or objects that could hinder safe landing

* The VIP aircraft parking bay must be checked and secured

* SPG protectees are legally exempt from personal security screening at all airports in India

* SPG protectees have access to VIP lounges at all AAI-operated airports

* Aircraft refuelling is conducted in strict accordance with Blue Book protocols

## **4.2 Rail Travel**

* An armed guard accompanies the VIP's train, stationed in a composite bogie adjacent to the VVIP saloon

* The VIP compartment is searched and anti-sabotage checked before journey commencement

* Sentries are posted on both sides of the saloon after the check and until the train departs

* At all halts, armed personnel are posted at rear and offside of the saloon

* Platforms at all non-halting stations are kept clear of crowds

* Local Police and Railway Police are responsible for security at halting stations

## **4.3 Camp/Residence Security**

* A Camp Commandant of appropriate rank is designated for each temporary residence (Raj Nivas, Guest House, private buildings)

* The layout is surveyed, adequate lighting arranged, and anti-sabotage checks conducted

* Catering staff are security vetted

* Officers and guards are deployed in outer, inner, and isolation cordon layers

* Door Frame Metal Detectors (DFMDs) and Hand-Held Metal Detectors (HHMDs) are installed at entry points

* Contingency exits and emergency motorcades are pre-arranged at each halt location

* Communication infrastructure (wireless, hotlines, fax) is set up at every camp

* Overhanging roofs, balconies, and trees — potential vantage points — are specifically identified and covered

# **5\. Multi-Agency Coordination Structure**

VVIP security is a layered, multi-agency responsibility. Understanding this hierarchy is essential for designing an AI monitoring system that correctly assigns roles, alerts, and data flows to the right stakeholders.

## **5.1 Agency Hierarchy and Responsibilities**

**Special Protection Group (SPG)**

* Sole mandate: proximate security of the Prime Minister

* Takes the final call on all security arrangements during the PM's movement

* Leads the ASL meeting and all planning

* Provides 40-60 officers in close proximity to the PM at all times

* Activates or cancels movement based on security assessment

**Intelligence Bureau (IB)**

* Provides actionable threat intelligence to SPG before and during visits

* Issues threat alerts and coordinates with state-level intelligence units

* Participates in ASL meetings

**State Police**

* Responsible for the overall security of the area being visited

* Gathers local intelligence; manages route clearance, venue sanitisation, and crowd management

* Deploys officers on route sectors, snipers at rooftops

* State DGP and Chief Secretary must be present when the PM arrives in the state

* Updates SPG on ground conditions; changes to the PM's movement are made only after state police confirmation

**District Magistrate (DM)**

* Coordinates civilian and administrative aspects of the visit

* Manages traffic diversions and public notification

* Member of the ASL coordination group

## **5.2 Communication Protocol**

All agencies operate on a secure, dedicated communication channel during VVIP movements. Key rules include:

* Nodal officers from each agency maintain real-time situational awareness

* All incidents, no matter how minor, are formally logged — these logs form part of institutional memory

* Signal jammers are active during transit to block mobile-triggered explosive devices

* Emergency communication lines (hotlines) are established between SPG, state DGP, DM, and IB

# **6\. Anti-Sabotage Framework**

Anti-sabotage precautions combined with access control are described in the Blue Book as 'the cornerstone of any security arrangement.' Three layered methods are mandated:

## **6.1 Three-Method Anti-Sabotage Check**

* Physical Search: Visual and manual inspection of all persons, vehicles, objects, and spaces

* Technical Gadgets: Explosive detectors, DFMD (Door Frame Metal Detectors), hand-held detectors, mine sweepers

* Sniffer Dogs: Trained dogs for explosive and contraband detection at venues, routes, and vehicles

## **6.2 Anti-Sabotage Scope**

* Camp/residence of the VVIP — full layout survey and sweep

* All vehicles in the convoy — mechanical check \+ anti-sabotage sweep

* The entire route — including drains, culverts, bridges, and underpasses

* All venues (public meetings, official events) — days in advance

* Catering and service staff — identity verification and security vetting

## **6.3 Modern and Emerging Threats Addressed**

* IED/remote explosive triggers: Countered by ECM signal jammers in the convoy's technical car

* Drone threats: Airspace monitoring protocols around VVIP locations

* Deepfakes and digital disinformation: Recently flagged as emerging threats (e.g. the Amit Shah deepfake incident highlighted in 2024\)

* Cyber threats: Digital communications and VVIP scheduling data are treated as sensitive intelligence

# **7\. Key Protocol Rules — Consolidated Reference**

The following are the most operationally critical rules from the Blue Book, extracted for direct use as system requirements for an AI-based monitoring platform:

* Rule 1 (Chapter 1): Overall responsibility for PM safety lies with the State Government. Proximate security is SPG's responsibility.

* Rule 2: State police must make all protective arrangements for security and safety of the PM.

* Rule 3: The convoy must not stop until it reaches its planned destination. An unplanned stop is a security breach.

* Rule 4: DGP and Chief Secretary must be present when the PM arrives in the state.

* Rule 5: All contingency plans must be exercised via physical rehearsal before the visit.

* Rule 6: All convoy vehicles must be of the same make and colour where possible.

* Rule 7: The SPG Director must give explicit clearance before convoy movement begins.

* Rule 8: State police update the SPG in real-time; any change in movement is SPG-approved only.

* Rule 9: Security personnel always face the crowd, never the VVIP.

* Rule 10: All incidents are formally logged; logs contribute to institutional memory.

## **8.1 Core Architecture Recommendations**

**Real-Time Data Layers**

* Live GPS tracking of all convoy vehicles with formation-integrity alerts (deviations from box formation)

* Traffic sensor integration for dynamic route assessment and automated diversion suggestions

* CCTV/video feed analysis at route chokepoints using computer vision for crowd density and anomaly detection

* Weather data feeds to trigger automatic contingency route activation (as per Blue Book weather protocols)

**Intelligence Integration**

* Secure API integration with IB threat alert systems

* NLP-based social media and open-source intelligence (OSINT) monitoring for crowd mobilisation or protest signals

* Pattern recognition on historical visit data to predict risk levels for specific routes, cities, and events

**Communication and Command**

* Role-based encrypted communication dashboard for SPG, State DGP, DM, and IB

* Automated alert escalation: anomaly detected → state police notified → SPG confirmation required

* Digital ASL meeting management with document sharing, route maps, and action tracking

**Compliance and Audit**

* Automated Blue Book compliance checklists for each visit phase (pre-visit, transit, venue, post-visit)

* Real-time breach detection: any deviation from protocol triggers an alert with the specific rule violated

* Post-visit debrief module for structured incident logging and institutional memory preservation

## **8.2 Key AI Algorithms and Techniques**

* Route Risk Scoring: Graph neural networks on road topology \+ historical incident data \+ real-time feeds

* Anomaly Detection: Isolation forest or LSTM-based models on convoy telemetry for unusual patterns

* Crowd Density Estimation: YOLOv8 or similar object detection on CCTV feeds

* Threat Classification: Transformer-based NLP models on intelligence reports and OSINT

* Predictive Rerouting: Reinforcement learning agent for real-time alternate route selection

* Deepfake Detection: Vision transformer models for authentication of video feeds

## **8.3 Integration Points with Blue Book Requirements**

* ASL Module: AI-generated risk assessments auto-shared with all ASL participants 72 hours pre-visit

* Convoy Monitor: Formation integrity checked against Blue Book convoy box standard every 5 seconds

* Route Clearance: AI flags unmarked vehicles on route and sends removal alerts to state police

* Contingency Trigger: System automatically activates Plan B route when primary route threat score exceeds threshold

* Compliance Dashboard: Real-time check against all 10 critical protocol rules during active movement

* Incident Logger: AI categorises, tags, and indexes every incident for future pattern analysis

# **10\. Conclusion and Development Priorities**

India's Blue Book represents one of the world's most detailed and systematically documented VVIP protection frameworks. Its layered approach — combining intelligence, physical security, technology, and multi-agency coordination — provides an ideal specification for an AI-based transport monitoring system.

The most critical development priorities for your AI system, derived from Blue Book protocol gaps and known failure points (such as the January 2022 Punjab security breach), are:

* Priority 1: Real-time convoy formation monitoring with instant alert to SPG if box integrity is compromised

* Priority 2: Predictive contingency route engine that activates automatically when primary route conditions degrade

* Priority 3: Multi-agency communication hub with role-based access, ensuring all stakeholders share a single operational picture

* Priority 4: Pre-visit ASL digitisation — replacing handwritten notes with AI-generated, indexed, and searchable visit records

* Priority 5: Crowd and perimeter AI using computer vision to detect anomalies before the convoy arrives at a location

* Priority 6: Compliance enforcement module to ensure no Blue Book rule is inadvertently bypassed

The system should be designed as a force multiplier for human security officers — not a replacement. All critical decisions (convoy movement, route changes, abort protocols) must remain with the SPG Director and authorised human commanders, with the AI providing actionable intelligence and real-time situational awareness to support those decisions.
